Stephen Hillenburg

Why He's Inspiring

Stephen Hillenburg was a marine biologist, animator, and writer born in 1961 in Oklahoma. Having a fascination with the ocean since a young age, Hillenburg
eventually became a professor at the Orange County Marine Institute, in part using comics to educate his students.

He contributed to the world of televised animation by working on the show Rocko's Modern Life, and creating his own show
Spongebob Squarepants, which became one of the biggest cartoons of the 90s and 2000s.

In 2017, he was unfortunately diagnosed with ALS, but despite his illness, he continued to work and contribute to the animation industry.
He passed a year later, in 2018.


The fact that he used his cartooning skills to help him educate his students, as well as his strong attitude to continue working through illness,
makes him a very inspiring person to me.
